Problem Statement: Audiobook Highlighting

People interested in listening to audiobooks want to have a way to highlight the text while listening to it, so they can go back and listen to it again. Our solution would make it easy to find and listen to whatever part of the recording somebody would wish to hear.

Affinity Diagram: Audiobook Highlighting

Our group put our minds together to conceptualize ideas based around highlighting audio books, whether it be fixing problems people would have or organizing what our sorting system for the highlights would look like. We also thought about sub-categories of highlights to easily sort them.

Typical Persona: Audiobook Highlighting

Our group worked to produce 4 personas that accurately portray a typical user of audibook highlighting. I reasoned english teachers would be a likely consumer of our services.

Storyboard Assignment: Audiobook Highlighting

Our group worked to produce 4 stories that are likely to be an actual use cases for our topic, audiobook highlighting. I continued to demonstrate how the app would be useful for teachers as for my persona I designed a teacher's profile.

Sketching Assignment: Audiobook Highlighting

This week, our group worked together to create at least 3 sketches each to conceptualize a graphical user inteface for our topic, audiobook highlighting. After we had all sketched, we chose our favorite aspects from all of the sketches and refined a final sketch.

Paper Prototyping Assignment: Audiobook Highlighting

This week, I created a paper prototype of how our audiobook highlighting to be integrated into a graphical user interface using only paper, hence, a paper prototype. I created a video showing an "iPad" in which I swapped several "screens" whilst interacting with my paper cutouts to simulate a user of our e-book highlighting feature.
